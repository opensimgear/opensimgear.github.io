key_specs:

- Compact puck-style tactile transducer.
- 8 ohm version; 16 ohm sibling also exists.
- Designed for small surfaces, seats, panels, and DIY tactile arrays.
- Lower output than full-size bass shakers.

compatibility:

- Requires small external amplifier.
- Works with audio LFE, SimHub, and telemetry-to-audio outputs.
- Useful in multi-zone arrays where size and cost matter.

software/driver ecosystem:

- Passive audio device.
- SimHub can address many pucks through multichannel sound cards and amplifiers.

strengths:

- Very compact and inexpensive.
- Good for localized pedal or seat-cushion cues.
- Multiple units can create finer spatial resolution.

limitations:

- Not enough output for heavy chassis or strong low-frequency motion feel.
- Needs many channels to become convincing.
- Retailer specs/prices should be rechecked before buying.

fit_notes:

- Best on light panels, pedal plates, and isolated cushion shells.
- Use higher-frequency effects like ABS, traction, and road texture rather than deep heave.
