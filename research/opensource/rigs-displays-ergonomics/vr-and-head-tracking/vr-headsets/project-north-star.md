- key_features:
  - Open-source files for Leap Motion's Project North Star AR headset.
  - Repository separates Electronics, Mechanical, and Software.
  - Hardware BOM, fabrication, assembly instructions, software setup, and calibration are linked from README.
  - Uses hand tracking and wide-FOV AR optics rather than opaque VR display.
- hardware_or_bom:
  - Headset mechanical parts, electronics package, display/optics components, and hand-tracking/controller elements as
    defined in project docs.
  - Community builds often involve 3D printing and custom assembly.
- firmware_or_software_stack:
  - Software setup/calibration in repository.
  - Monado lists North Star support on Linux with Intel RealSense T265 or Monado Basalt tracking paths.
- build_requirements:
  - 3D printing/fabrication, electronics assembly, optics alignment, Leap Motion/Ultraleap tracking setup, calibration.
- compatibility:
  - AR/XR development, Unity/OpenXR/SteamVR-style experimentation depending on software stack [uncertain].
  - Less direct fit for conventional sim racing than VR HMDs because it is see-through AR hardware.
- strengths:
  - Important open AR headset reference with real mechanical/electronics/software source.
  - Lets builders study headset ergonomics, optics, and tracking integration.
  - Can pair with Monado for open XR runtime experiments.
- limitations:
  - Not a current consumer product.
  - Requires difficult optics/electronics sourcing and calibration.
  - License reporting conflict between GitHub and older blog/docs should be resolved before reuse.
