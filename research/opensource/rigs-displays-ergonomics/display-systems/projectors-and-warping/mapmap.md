- project: MapMap
- maintainer_or_org: MapMap team
- component_category: display-systems
- subcategory: projection mapping software

- key_features:
  - Free/open-source video mapping software for Windows, macOS, and Linux.
  - Lets users map video/image content onto irregular surfaces using projected geometry.
  - Supports projection-mapping workflows for artists and small teams.
- hardware_or_bom:
  - Requires PC/Mac/Linux machine and projector treated as an additional display.
  - No sim-specific hardware BOM.
- firmware_or_software_stack:
  - C++/Qt-style desktop application with media/projection mapping pipeline [exact dependencies should be checked in
    INSTALL.md].
- build_requirements:
  - Follow INSTALL.md from source; captured README notes GStreamer for macOS.
  - Projector alignment and display-configuration setup.
- compatibility:
  - Useful for single-projector/quasi-artistic mapping around simulator shells, dashboards, or physical cockpit props.
  - Not a full edge-blending/auto-calibration suite for serious multi-projector domes.
- strengths:
  - Easier entry point than research-grade mapping stacks.
  - Cross-platform and GPL.
  - Good for testing projection ideas before investing in commercial warp/blend.
- limitations:
  - Not simulator-specific.
  - Limited direct evidence of current maintenance.
  - Advanced edge blending, black-level correction, and camera auto-calibration may require other tools.
- commercial_analogs:
  - MadMapper
  - Resolume Arena mapping workflows
  - HeavyM
- fit_notes:
  - Better for cockpit ambiance, panels, or small projection surfaces than primary 180-degree simulator visuals.
  - Pair with native sim display output capture if used for actual outside visuals.
