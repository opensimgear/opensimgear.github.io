project: OpenHornet

maintainer_or_org: OpenHornet community / `jrsteensen`

key_features:

- 1:1 replica F/A-18C Lot 20 cockpit for home entertainment.
- Integrates with DCS F/A-18C through DCS-BIOS.
- Fully backlit panels with acrylic backplate/light plate and Rowmark legend plate.
- HOTAS section includes Thrustmaster grip-compatible stick mechanics and a functional replica throttle with
  solenoid-driven detents.
- Electronics system, ABSIS, runs on Arduino/ESP32 microcontrollers over USB and RS-485 buses.

hardware_or_bom:

- MDF cockpit structure, PETG/resin printed console surfaces and brackets, acrylic/Rowmark panel stack, aluminum plates
  for stick/throttle, Arduinos, ESP32s, 12 V/5 V/3.3 V buses, RS-485 wiring.

firmware_or_software_stack:

- DCS-BIOS plus OpenHornet Arduino Based Simulator Interface System sketches.

build_requirements:

- Advanced fabrication: MDF woodworking, acrylic cutting/engraving, 3D printing, aluminum work, electronics, firmware
  uploads, DCS-BIOS setup.

compatibility:

- DCS: World F/A-18C Hornet module.

strengths:

- Deep, coherent open cockpit ecosystem.
- Strong fit for panel, switch, and cockpit interface research.
- Includes cockpit structure, panels, electronics, and HOTAS mechanics.

limitations:

- Non-commercial license.
- Large, expensive, time-heavy build.
- Not a general-purpose button box or generic HOTAS.

fit_notes:
