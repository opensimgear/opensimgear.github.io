# DIY Sim Racing FFB Pedal

- project: DIY Sim Racing FFB Pedal
- maintainer_or_org: ChrGri and contributors
- component_category: pedals
- subcategory: active force-feedback pedal
- maturity_or_status: Large active DIY project with firmware, SimHub plugin, mechanical design, PCB repositories, wiki,
  Discord, and build automation.
- license: CC BY-NC-SA 4.0; source-available/noncommercial, not OSI-open because of NC restriction.
- repo_url: https://github.com/ChrGri/DIY-Sim-Racing-FFB-Pedal
- docs_url: https://github.com/ChrGri/DIY-Sim-Racing-FFB-Pedal/wiki
- source_urls:
  - https://github.com/ChrGri/DIY-Sim-Racing-FFB-Pedal
  - https://github.com/ChrGri/DIY-Sim-Racing-FFB-Pedal-Mechanical-Design
  - https://github.com/gilphilbert/DIY-Sim-Racing-FFB-Pedal-PCBs
  - https://github.com/ChrGri/DIY-Sim-Racing-FFB-Pedal/wiki
- key_features:
  - Servo-driven active pedal using linear rail and high-power actuator behavior.
  - Adjustable brake pressure, travel, response, throttle weight/stiffness, and car-specific profiles.
  - Feedback types include ABS, traction control, RPM vibration, road rumble, and other SimHub/game effects.
  - USB or wireless connectivity, analog output option, custom PCBs, SimHub plugin.
- hardware_or_bom:
  - Mechanical pedal assembly with servo, linear rail, drive electronics, control board, power board, sensors, and
    enclosure.
  - Separate repos cover mechanical/electrical design and PCB assemblies.
- firmware_or_software_stack:
  - Firmware, SimHub plugin, control-loop code, web flash tool, and wiki documentation.
  - Hardware/design options documented across wiki and Discord.
- build_requirements:
  - Advanced mechatronics project with high-force moving parts.
  - Requires servo/power electronics, PCB build or purchase, firmware flashing, SimHub integration, mechanical
    alignment, and safety controls.
- compatibility:
  - PC sim racing through USB/wireless plus SimHub/game telemetry.
  - Analog output can integrate into other devices.
- strengths:
  - Closest open/source-available analog to premium active pedals.
  - Large community, multiple repositories, and serious firmware/plugin ecosystem.
  - Can create effects impossible on passive load-cell pedals.
- limitations:
  - License forbids commercial use.
  - Safety risk: powered pedal can injure if assembled/tuned badly.
  - More expensive and complex than passive load-cell pedals.
- commercial_analogs:
  - Simucube ActivePedal Pro/Ultimate
  - MOZA mBooster Active Pedal
  - Simagic P1000/P2000 haptic reactor setups [partial analog]
- fit_notes:
  - Best for expert builders who specifically want active pedal feel and telemetry feedback.
