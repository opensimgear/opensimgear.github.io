# OpenSourceSimWheelESP32

- project: OpenSourceSimWheelESP32
- maintainer_or_org: afpineda
- component_category: steering-wheels
- subcategory: wireless steering-wheel/button-box electronics
- maturity_or_status: Active, documented, fully operational project with releases and extensive documentation.
- license: EUPL-1.2
- repo_url: https://github.com/afpineda/OpenSourceSimWheelESP32
- docs_url: https://afpineda.github.io/OpenSourceSimWheelESP32/
- source_urls:
  - https://github.com/afpineda/OpenSourceSimWheelESP32
  - https://afpineda.github.io/OpenSourceSimWheelESP32/
- key_features:
  - Open hardware/software for ESP32 sim-racing wheel or button box.
  - BLE wireless and USB wired modes.
  - Up to 128 switches/buttons, alternate mode, configurable button maps, funky switch/directional pad handling, and
    adjustable rotary encoder pulse width.
  - Dual clutch paddles can act as F1 clutch, launch control, alternate mode, buttons, or analog axes.
  - SimHub telemetry for RGB LEDs, single-color LEDs, OLEDs, and notifications.
- hardware_or_bom:
  - ESP32/ESP32-S3 boards, rechargeable battery or wheel-base/USB power, button matrices, multiplexers, GPIO expanders,
    shift registers, rotary encoders, funky switches, clutch paddles, optional display/LED hardware.
  - Includes PCB designs and auxiliary modules.
- firmware_or_software_stack:
  - C++/Arduino ESP32 firmware.
  - Companion app for preferences.
  - SimHub plugin for telemetry and basic preferences.
- build_requirements:
  - ESP32 flashing workflow, PCB or perfboard wiring, battery/power handling, and SimHub setup for telemetry.
  - Mechanical wheel/rim/button-plate design still required unless using an existing design.
- compatibility:
  - PC over BLE HID or USB.
  - SimHub telemetry ecosystem.
  - Can be adapted to many wheel designs or standalone button boxes.
- strengths:
  - Strong fit for open steering-wheel electronics.
  - Wireless, configurable, documented, and current.
  - Better software engineering and feature depth than most one-off Arduino wheels.
- limitations:
  - Not a complete mechanical rim by itself.
  - Wireless reliability and battery packaging depend on builder execution.
  - SimHub required for telemetry features.
- commercial_analogs:
  - Cube Controls wireless/USB wheels
  - Simucube Wireless Wheel ecosystem
  - Ascher Racing USB wheels
  - Fanatec Podium Button Module-style wheel electronics
- fit_notes:
  - Best open electronics platform for custom button plates, GT/formula wheels, and swappable DIY rims.
