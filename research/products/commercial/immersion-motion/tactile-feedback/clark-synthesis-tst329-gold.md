- Premium tactile sound transducer below TST429 Platinum.
- Official comparison says TST429 produces about twice the output of TST329.
- Installation guide recommends 125-150 W at 4 ohm for TST329/TST429 class units.
- Designed for seats, risers, platforms, simulators, and audio applications.

compatibility:

- Requires external amplifier.
- Works with audio LFE, SimHub, and other telemetry-to-audio tools.

software/driver ecosystem:

- Passive transducer; software depends on audio source.
- Fine filtering is useful because the unit can reproduce more than low bass.

strengths:

- Premium tactile fidelity without moving to TST429 cost/output class.
- More flexible mounting than TST429 per Clark installation notes.
- Strong fit for high-quality seat or cockpit texture channel.

limitations:

- Less sim-specific packaging than ButtKicker kits.
- Price must be checked with retailer before purchase.
- Amplifier matching and filtering are buyer responsibility.
