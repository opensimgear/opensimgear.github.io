- key_features:
  - 3D printable sequential shifter designed for strength, longevity, and low cost.
  - Uses two limit switches and Arduino Pro Micro USB joystick output.
  - Adjustable travel stoppers, springs, and optional ball plungers for feel.
  - Mounting pattern documented for rig brackets.
- hardware_or_bom:
  - Printed body, shaft, cover, switch spacers, optional knob/pivot parts.
  - Pivot tube or M12 bolt, two roller-lever limit switches, Arduino Pro Micro, M3/M10/M12 hardware, springs, ball
    plungers, wiring, USB cable.
- firmware_or_software_stack:
  - Arduino sketch using MHeironimus Joystick Library.
- build_requirements:
  - 3D printing about 500 g filament and roughly 1.5 days print time per maintainer.
  - Basic soldering, Arduino flashing, spring/limit-switch adjustment, rig mounting.
- compatibility:
  - PC USB joystick, mapped as shift up/down in simulator.
- strengths:
  - Clear assembly docs and simple electronics.
  - Realistic sequential lever ergonomics with cheap parts.
  - Good first DIY shifter project.
- limitations:
  - No explicit license.
  - Sequential only.
  - 3D printed PLA durability depends on print settings and driving force.

- fit_notes:
