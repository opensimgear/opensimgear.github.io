## Intro

Selecting a motor for a ball-screw linear actuator requires more than matching peak force. The full motion cycle
matters: inertial torque during acceleration, RMS heating over repeated cycles, speed at the target stroke rate, and
whether the rotor inertia is a sensible match for the load.

This calculator builds that full picture from your motion profile, load, axis orientation, screw geometry, and
transmission, then evaluates every motor in the database against peak torque, RMS torque, and speed with a configurable
safety factor.

### How to use

1. **Motion Profile** — Set stroke, peak velocity, acceleration, deceleration, and dwell time. The calculator computes
   the trapezoidal timing automatically and flags short-stroke triangular moves.
2. **System** — Choose Single Axis, 4-Actuator Platform, or 6-Actuator Stewart Platform. Multi-actuator modes distribute
   force per actuator, including the Stewart angle projection.
3. **Load** — Enter moving mass, friction, external load, guide preload, and an imbalance factor for uneven force
   sharing.
4. **Axis** — Set horizontal, vertical, or inclined orientation. Gravity is applied automatically for non-horizontal
   axes. Enable holding if the actuator must support load at rest.
5. **Ball Screw** — Choose a common screw size or enter custom pitch and diameter. Screw mass and rotational inertia
   update live.
6. **Transmission** — Add gear ratio, efficiency, and drivetrain inertia for belt or gearbox reductions. Direct drive is
   `1.0:1`.
7. **Safety** — Safety factor scales all required values before motors are checked.
8. **Results table** — Re-sort by status, score, torque margin, speed margin, or inertia ratio. Hover a row for a
   detailed breakdown of required torque, speed, power, and inertia.

### Engineering notes

- **Peak torque** is checked against the motor peak rating and occurs during acceleration when load torque and inertial
  torque stack.
- **RMS torque** is checked against rated continuous torque and represents thermal loading over the full move-and-dwell
  cycle.
- **Inertia ratio** above `10:1` is treated as a warning because it usually hurts responsiveness and tuning margin.
- **Negative deceleration torque** is valid regenerative behavior and still contributes to RMS heating through `T²`.
- **Fit score** rewards motors that satisfy the constraints without being dramatically oversized.
- In **Stewart** mode, the motion-profile inputs are treated as actuator-axis values. The calculator applies angle-based
  load sharing, but it does not derive actuator travel from platform heave or pose kinematics.
